About
The Table
◆

Yuko cooks the way she reads — slowly, attentively, with the instinct that everything connects if you hold it long enough. Her dinners happen in a room surrounded by thirty years of books, records, and accumulated evidence of a life spent paying attention.

Guests sit close together. The food is Japanese in its bones and New York in its accidents. She does not follow a fixed menu — she follows what arrived at the market that morning, what felt right in the afternoon.

Eight seats. One table. No phones at dinner.
